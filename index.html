Benefits of Scouting

Teaches valuable life skills

Scouts provides a multitude of opportunities and activities which teach people skills for life. With a whole array of badges to complete and camps during the year, people can learn and explore a plethora of new and fun skills. From cooking to watersports and expeditions,there's something for everyone to learn and enjoy.


Helps you grow and improve as a person

Scouts presents people with a variety of challenges and beneficial life lessons, all of which help improve confidence in yourself and your abilities, as well as helping you grow into someone with the desire and ability to make the world a better place. It helps you achieve things you never thought possible, and even when faced witha difficult problem, scouts helps develop your perseverance and determination.


Builds strong relationship and leadership skills

Scouts allows you o engage with other scouts and the wider community, and offers innumerable evets and activities which all help develop and nurture leadership skills, an invaluable asset to have no matter where or whoyou want to be in the future..


But most importantly...

IT'S FUN!!!




  Camps and Expeditions

Camps and Expeditions

GSAL scouts have 2 main camps per year. The annual Summer Camp alternates between two beautiful field campsites in Wales and the Yorksire Dales, and takes place for 10 days during July. The smaller Patrol camps take place throughout the year on the GSAL campsite, and last 2 nights.


Scouts also take part in expeditions, which invole hiking and setting up camp in a different place for at least 1 night. These can be challenging mentaly and physically, but are very fun and rewarding as well.



  Badges

Badges

Scouts can complete activities to earn badges. There are 4 types of badges: Challenge badges, which are the main badges which scouts aspire to get, Activity badges, which you can earn by completing various activities, ranging from flying to art to geocaching to science. There are also staged badges, which have different levels to complete as you advance through them. The final type of badges are core badges, which show your membership, patrol and rank.




  Uniform

Uniform

The scout uniform consists of a green shirt and a necker with a woggle, which is essentially a small band to hold the necker in place. Badges, once earned, are sewn onto the scout uniform. From the ages of 14-18, you become an explorer scout, and the only difference in uniform is that the shirt is a khaki brown colour.
